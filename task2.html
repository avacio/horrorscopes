Summary

Reasoning:

  • Did the dogs even have a choice?
    • Did they want to risk their dog-day lives for space?
  • It makes me sad to think about all of these dogs being sacrificed for the 'advancement of science' so I wanted to make a visualization where all these dogs could play together peacefully.
  • I tried to make a "floating animation" where these space dogs could all just be chilling in space.
    • For the nauseous, you can turn off the animation by pressing the 'FLOAT ON/OFF' button.
  • To see the actual fates of all these beautiful dogs, please click the 'SHOW FATE' button.
  • You can hover over the dogs to get a tooltip with their names and their flights, and any notes.

Attributes:

  • Dog gender ==> COLOR of space outfit
  • Number of flights (space missions!) a dog embarked on ==> SIZE of dog
  • Fate (did the dog survive all the flights or not) ==> angel ICON with halo and wings if they died in action

Process

While not necessary to display, I have found it extremely conducive to my own learning process to document the process of how I got to this point!

Initial Brainstorming:

  • Small multiples like the film flowers example
    • Could be dog collar designs!!
  • Attributes to use:
    • Name (English)
      • Maybe only as a tooltip
    • Gender
      • Color or collar shape
    • Flights
      • Outline of dogtag?
      • Could be number of sides to the dogtag (i.e. circle = no flights, and hexagon = 6 flights)
    • Fate
      • Died or survived
        • Spatial regions? Pet cemetery
        • Skeleton/ghost icon
      • Linked view of time of death
  • Possible interactions and animations:
    • Select dog collars in the doghouse to show those dogs in the race...
    • Dog race/relay with the collars
      • Survivors make it past the finish line in the animation...
    • Add notes to tooltip as well

Iterations:

  • Initially I thought I would only make a "doghouse" with different collar types.
  • Collar color denotes gender, while the dog tag shape itself would denote the quantiative value of number of flights
  • With this design, I planned to have a linked view of a "dog race" of sorts...
  • Using dates of the flights and survival status it would be reminiscent of dog races.
  • I was not altogether comfortable with this design though, because it implies that there are 'winner' and 'loser' dogs, when in reality they are all winners. :)
  • The x-scale of time is also misleading because intuitively it tells readers that the younger dogs (embarking on later flights) are 'faster' when this is not the case at all.
  • Either way, I thought I'd prototype this to see if it was feasible.

  • I found some incredibly cute pictures of dog collars, and played with the idea of dog tag size representing the number of flights
  • Then I tried to whip up a linked view, but something felt off...
  • The way I was showing the missions/sacrifices of these dogs as 'races' felt dehumanizing (or de-canine-izing) and I didn't like it.
  • That's where I ended up with my current version where the dogs are just chilling, floating in space!
  • They don't even have to be dead or alive, and the "Show Fate" button can be toggled as to viewer preference
